EFC-D: Energy-Flow Dynamics

The local energy-flow potential \( E_f \) is defined as a function of mass density \( \rho \) and entropy \( S \):

\[ E_f = \rho (1 - S). \]

Interpretation:

  • high density and low entropy \(\Rightarrow\) large \(E_f\);
  • high entropy suppresses \(E_f\).

Density

The mass density is

\[ \rho = \frac{m}{V}. \]

Energy-Flow Rate

The temporal change in the energy-flow potential is written as

\[ \frac{dE_f}{dt} = \nabla_t E_f. \]

EFC-S: Entropy Field & Gradient

EFC starts from a thermodynamic field \( S(\mathbf{x}) \) representing the local entropy state of the matter–information configuration.

Entropy Field

The entropy field is modeled as

\[ S(\mathbf{x}) \in [0,1], \]

where

  • \( S = 0 \): highly ordered regions;
  • \( S = 1 \): high-entropy regions.

Entropy Gradient

The entropy gradient drives anisotropic flow:

\[ \nabla S(\mathbf{x}) = \left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial S}{\partial y}, \frac{\partial S}{\partial z} \right). \]

Its magnitude is

\[ \lvert \nabla S \rvert = \sqrt{ \left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial x} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial y} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial z} \right)^2 }. \]

s0 → s1 Light Propagation Dynamics

Entropy Endpoints

We define two entropy endpoints:

  • \( s_0 \): low-entropy, high-structure endpoint;
  • \( s_1 \): high-entropy, high-diffusion endpoint.

The midpoint and entropy span are

\[ S_{\text{mid}} = \tfrac{1}{2}(s_0 + s_1), \qquad \Delta S = s_1 - s_0. \]

The normalized entropy coordinate is

\[ x(S) = \frac{S - S_{\text{mid}}}{\Delta S/2}. \]

Effective Speed of Light

The effective speed of light is modeled as

\[ c(S) = c_0 \left( 1 + a_{\text{edge}}\, x(S)^2 \right), \]

where \( a_{\text{edge}} \) controls the strength of edge-driven variation.

Light Travel Time

For a photon propagating along a path \( \gamma \), the observed travel time is

\[ t_{\text{obs}} = \int_{\gamma} \frac{dl}{c(S(l))}. \]

Lensing Behaviour

Low-entropy endpoints near \( s_0 \) act as focusing wells, while high-entropy regions near \( s_1 \) generate large-scale defocusing. This creates a dual-lens structure consistent with halo asymmetries.

Observables in Energy-Flow Cosmology

All observable quantities are modulated by the entropy field \( S(\mathbf{x}) \), the energy-flow field \( E_f(\mathbf{x}) \), and the entropy-dependent light speed \( c(S) \).

Light Propagation

The light-travel time along a photon path \( \gamma \) is

\[ t_{\text{obs}} = \int_{\gamma} \frac{dl}{c(S(l))}. \]

Lensing

In GR, lensing depends only on the mass distribution. In EFC it depends on both mass and entropy structure:

\[ \text{Lensing} = \text{Mass Distribution} + S(\mathbf{x}) + c(S). \]

Rotation Curves

Circular velocities follow from the potential \( \Phi \):

\[ v(r) = \sqrt{\, r \, \frac{\partial \Phi}{\partial r} \, }. \]

Expansion Rate

The EFC expansion rate is

\[ H(E_f, S) = \sqrt{|E_f|}\,(1 + S). \]

Dependency Chain

The core dependency chain is

\[ S(\mathbf{x}) \;\Rightarrow\; E_f(\mathbf{x}) \;\Rightarrow\; \Phi(E_f, S) \;\Rightarrow\; c(S) \;\Rightarrow\; H(E_f, S) \;\Rightarrow\; \text{Observables}. \]
