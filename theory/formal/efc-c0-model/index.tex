\section*{EFC–C₀: Entropy–Information Base Layer}

This layer defines how entropy controls information capacity, 
propagation resistance, and effective signal speed.

\subsection*{1. Information Capacity}

\[
C(x) = C_0\,\bigl(1 - S(x)\bigr).
\]

Low entropy gives high capacity; high entropy reduces capacity.

\subsection*{2. Propagation Resistance}

\[
R(x) = R\!\left(S(x),\,\rho(x)\right).
\]

Resistance increases with entropy and modifies all propagation behaviour.

\subsection*{3. Effective Speed}

\[
c_{\mathrm{eff}}(x) = \frac{1}{1 + R(x)}.
\]

This expression defines variable signal speed in the s₀/s₁ model.

\subsection*{4. Regimes}

\[
S_0: \text{low-entropy regime}, \qquad
S_1: \text{high-entropy regime}.
\]

These two reference points bracket the propagation-window of EFC.
