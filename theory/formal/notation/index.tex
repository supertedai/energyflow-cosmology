\section*{EFC Notation: Master Specification}

\subsection*{1. Purpose}

This document defines the global notation system used across all formal
models in Energy-Flow Cosmology (EFC). It standardises mathematical
symbols, tensor/field naming, derivative conventions and component
structure to ensure consistency across the S, D, H, C$_0$, and
formal-spec modules.

\subsection*{2. Core Scalar Fields}

\[
\begin{aligned}
S(\mathbf{x},t) &:&\;\text{entropy field, normalized } 0 \leq S \leq 1 \\
\rho(\mathbf{x},t) &:&\;\text{structural density} \\
E_f(\mathbf{x},t) &:&\;\text{energy–flow potential} \\
R(\mathbf{x},t) &:&\;\text{grid resistance} \\
C(\mathbf{x},t) &:&\;\text{information capacity} \\
I_0(\mathbf{x},t) &:&\;\text{information resonance scalar}
\end{aligned}
\]

\subsection*{3. Derived Quantities}

Energy–flow potential:
\[
E_f = \rho(1-S)
\]

Information capacity:
\[
C = C_0(1-S)
\]

Propagation speed:
\[
c_{\text{eff}} = (1 + R)^{-1}
\]

Information resonance:
\[
I_0 = \gamma |E_f \cdot \nabla S|
\]

\subsection*{4. Gradient and Differential Operators}

Spatial gradients:
\[
\nabla S, \quad \nabla \rho, \quad \nabla E_f
\]

Temporal evolution operator:
\[
\frac{d}{dt}
    = \frac{\partial}{\partial t}
    + \mathbf{v}\cdot\nabla
\]

Directional derivative:
\[
\nabla_{\mathbf{v}} f = \mathbf{v}\cdot\nabla f
\]

\subsection*{5. Vector and Direction Symbols}

Propagation direction:
\[
\hat{\mathbf{u}}
\]

Propagation velocity:
\[
\mathbf{v} = c_{\text{eff}}\hat{\mathbf{u}}
\]

\subsection*{6. Canonical Update Ordering}

\[
S \rightarrow \rho \rightarrow E_f \rightarrow R \rightarrow c_{\text{eff}}
\]

\[
S \rightarrow (C,\nabla S) \rightarrow I_0
\]

This notation specification guarantees uniform mathematical expression
across:

\begin{itemize}
    \item EFC–S (Spatial Structure)
    \item EFC–D (Dynamics)
    \item EFC–H (Halo / Emergent Mass)

interpretation of all EFC formal documents.
