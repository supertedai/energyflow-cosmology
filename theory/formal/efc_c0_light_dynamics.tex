\section{EFC-C\textsubscript{0}: Entropy-Dependent Light Propagation}
\label{sec:efc_c0}

Energy-Flow Cosmology treats the effective speed of light as an
emergent property of the thermodynamic state of the grid. The
field $S(\mathbf{x})$ modulates structure, energy-flow and the
propagation of information.

The closure of the dynamical chain is:
\[
  S(\mathbf{x})
  \;\Rightarrow\;
  E_f(\mathbf{x})
  \;\Rightarrow\;
  \Phi(E_f,S)
  \;\Rightarrow\;
  c(S)
  \;\Rightarrow\;
  H(E_f,S)
  \;\Rightarrow\;
  \text{Observables}.
\]

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Entropy Endpoints and Normalised Coordinate}

Two entropy endpoints are defined:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $s_0$: low-entropy, high-structure endpoint,
  \item $s_1$: high-entropy, high-diffusion endpoint.
\end{itemize}

Midpoint and entropy span:
\[
  S_{\text{mid}} = \frac{1}{2}(s_0 + s_1),
  \qquad
  \Delta S = s_1 - s_0.
\]

The normalised coordinate becomes:
\[
  x(S) = \frac{S - S_{\text{mid}}}{\Delta S / 2}.
\]

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Effective Speed of Light}

The effective speed of light:
\[
  c(S) = c_0 \left( 1 + a_{\text{edge}} x(S)^2 \right).
\]

Corresponding refractive index:
\[
  n(S) = \frac{1}{1 + a_{\text{edge}} x(S)^2}.
\]

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Light Travel Time}

Observed travel time for a photon path $\gamma$:
\[
  t_{\text{obs}}
  = \int_{\gamma} \frac{dl}{c(S(l))}
  = \frac{1}{c_0} \int_{\gamma} n(S(l)) dl.
\]

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Dual Focusing--Defocusing Behaviour}

\begin{itemize}
  \item $s_0$: local focusing by entropy-controlled refractive index,
  \item $s_1$: broad-scale defocusing,
  \item $S_{\text{mid}}$: slowest propagation and maximal time delays.
\end{itemize}

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Coupling to EFC Dynamics}

Because $S(\mathbf{x})$ modulates:
\[
E_f = \rho (1-S),
\qquad
H(E_f,S) = \sqrt{|E_f|} (1+S),
\]
the same entropy field governs structure, expansion, and light
propagation. Apparent anomalies in lensing, time delays and expansion
rates can therefore emerge from entropy-structured light propagation
rather than additional dark components.
