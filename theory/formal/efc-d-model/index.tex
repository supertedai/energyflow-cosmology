\section{EFC-D: Energy-Flow Dynamics}

The dynamic layer describes how the energy–flow field $E_f$ evolves in space and time.
It connects structural quantities from EFC-S with entropy-driven suppression from EFC-C₀.

\subsection{Energy–Flow Potential}

The local potential is defined as:
\[
E_f = \rho (1 - S).
\]

Density:
\[
\rho = \frac{m}{V}.
\]

Interpretation:
\begin{itemize}
    \item High density and low entropy $\Rightarrow$ strong energy–flow.
    \item Increasing entropy reduces the local flow capacity.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Temporal Evolution}

The time derivative of the flow potential is:
\[
\frac{dE_f}{dt} = \nabla_t E_f
                 = (1-S)\frac{d\rho}{dt}
                   - \rho \frac{dS}{dt}.
\]

\subsection{Spatial Flow}

The spatial gradient is:
\[
\nabla E_f = (1 - S)\nabla\rho - \rho \nabla S.
\]

The direction of energy flow is determined by:
\[
\vec{v}_f = -\nabla E_f.
\]

\subsection{Radial Dynamics Around a Halo}

Given a spherically symmetric profile:

\[
E_f(r) = \rho(r)\,\big(1 - S(r)\big),
\]

the radial acceleration is:

\[
a(r) = -\frac{dE_f}{dr}
     = -\left[(1-S)\frac{d\rho}{dr} - \rho \frac{dS}{dr}\right].
\]

This predicts:
\begin{itemize}
    \item flat rotation curves when $\rho'(r)$ and $S'(r)$ balance,
    \item lensing proportional to $E_f(r)$,
    \item structural stability from the entropy-gradient term.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Dynamic Update Rule}

The evolution equation is:

\[
\boxed{
\frac{\partial E_f}{\partial t}
= (1-S)\,\frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t}
  - \rho\,\frac{\partial S}{\partial t}
  - \vec{v}_f \cdot \nabla E_f
}
\]

with the flow field:

\[
\vec{v}_f = -\nabla E_f.
\]

This completes the formal dynamic layer of EFC.
