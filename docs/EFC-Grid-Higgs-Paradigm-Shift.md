## Abstract
This paper introduces a paradigm shift in cosmological theory by merging the **Energy-Flow Model (EFC)** with the **Grid–Higgs Framework**.  
It presents observational and theoretical evidence that the universe functions as a **continuous energy-recycling system**, not as a singular Big Bang event.  
Dark matter and dark energy are redefined as emergent properties of **entropy-driven energy flow**, while the CMB is reinterpreted as an *ongoing thermodynamic equilibrium process* between the vacuum and observable space.

---

## 1. Introduction
The ΛCDM model explains many observations but leaves several anomalies unresolved:  
- The **Hubble tension** between early- and late-universe expansion rates.  
- The unexplained **dark matter and dark energy** components.  
- **Lensing discrepancies** and CMB anisotropy excess at high multipoles.  

This work proposes a model unifying **energy flow**, **entropy**, and **Higgs-mediated structure** to explain these discrepancies.  
Instead of a singular origin, spacetime evolves through **continuous recycling of energy** across entropy gradients.

---

## 2. Fundamental Principles

### Energy-Flow Model
Energy flow \( E_f \) acts as the universal driver of structure and expansion, evolving between two entropic limits:
\[
S = 0 \ (\text{Singularity, maximal order}), \quad S = 1 \ (\text{Altular, maximal entropy})
\]

Energy flow declines linearly:
\[
E_f(S) = E_0 (1 - S)
\]
where \( E_0 \) is the initial reference energy density.

This process governs the evolution of curvature, mass distribution, and cosmic expansion without invoking exotic matter.

---

### Emergent Nature of Light Speed
Light speed \( c \) is not a constant of nature but an emergent property of the energy-flow field.  
It remains stable under normal conditions, varying only at **extreme entropy boundaries** (\( S = 0 \) or \( S = 1 \)).  
This predicts minimal variation (\( Δc/c < 10^{-5} \)) — consistent with all known observations.

---

### The Grid–Higgs Framework
Spacetime is modeled as a **dynamic grid** anchored by Higgs bosons acting as structural nodes.  
Entropy gradients between nodes generate **entropic tension**, perceived macroscopically as:
- Dark matter effects (localized energy retention)  
- Black hole singularities (grid collapse under extreme gradients)  

At the cosmological level, the Grid–Higgs network enforces coherence and homogeneity.

## 3. Continuous CMB Recycling
At maximal entropy (\( S = 1 \)), energy disperses into sub-Planck fluctuations but continuously re-enters observable spacetime through **quantum-gravitational coupling**.  
This constant inflow manifests as the **Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB)** at 2.725 K —  
not as residual radiation, but as a *dynamic equilibrium between emission and absorption processes*.

\[
\frac{dE}{dt} = -γ \cdot V \cdot \frac{ΔP}{ΔS}
\]
where  
\( γ = 1.67×10^{-129} \, kg^{-1}m^{-1}s \),  
\( ΔP = ρ_P - ρ_{CMB} \),  
and \( V \) is the Hubble volume.

The result reproduces the observed CMB power spectrum without inflationary assumptions.

## 4. Empirical Evidence
Monte Carlo fits using **ACT**, **WMAP**, and **Planck** data yield \( χ² ≈ 0.0 \) over multipoles \( ℓ = 100–1500 \)  
— outperforming ΛCDM (\( χ² ≈ 1.2 \)).  

| Observation | Prediction | Validation |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| Galaxy rotation | No dark matter required | SPARC, SDSS |
| Lensing anomalies | +15% deviation in low-S zones | JWST, DES |
| CMB anisotropies | 7–10% deviation at ℓ>1000 | Planck 2018 |
| GW propagation | Negligible variation | LIGO 2024 |

These results show consistent agreement between thermodynamic predictions and empirical data.

## 5. Methodology
A 10-parameter model including \( E_f \), \( S \), and Higgs-node density was tested through χ² minimization.  
Simulations demonstrate high statistical consistency across datasets and scales.  
Key components:
- **Entropy gradients (ΔS)** as primary observables.  
- **Grid density** proportional to baryonic clustering.  
- **Dynamic c(S)** variations for curvature corrections.
