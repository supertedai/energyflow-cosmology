## Abstract
This study proposes a unified hypothesis where **energy flow** governs the emergence and evolution of spacetime, and **entropy** drives cosmic dynamics.  
Unlike ΛCDM, which separates dark matter and dark energy, this model presents a **single thermodynamic Grid**—a Higgs-based energy structure in which both phenomena emerge naturally from entropy-regulated flow.  
The framework predicts measurable effects in redshift anomalies, gravitational lensing, and the CMB, offering a testable path toward a thermodynamic interpretation of gravity and expansion.

---

## 1. Introduction
ΛCDM explains many observations yet leaves unresolved:
- The **Hubble tension**
- The nature of **dark matter and dark energy**
- The **fine-tuning** of Λ  
This hypothesis extends *Energy-Flow Cosmology (EFC)* by describing how **entropy-driven energy flow** forms spacetime structure.  
Dark matter arises as **non-resonant energy states** in the Higgs Grid; dark energy emerges from **global entropy gradients** driving expansion.

## 2. Theoretical Foundations

### 2.1 Energy Flow as the Primary Driver
Energy flow \(E_f\) sustains spacetime stability through a linear decline with entropy:
\[
E_f(S) = E_0(1 - S)
\]
where \(S = 0\) denotes singular order and \(S = 1\) full dispersion.  
Expansion correlates directly with declining \(E_f\).  

### 2.2 The Speed of Light as Emergent
Light speed \(c\) arises from the **energy-flow gradient**, not as a universal constant.  
At high energy density (\(S = 0\)), \(c\) loses meaning; at maximal entropy (\(S = 1\)), spacetime collapses.

### 2.3 Dark Matter from Higgs Non-Resonance
Dark matter represents **non-resonant states** of the Higgs Grid that fail to interact electromagnetically.  
These sub-Planck fluctuations stabilize galaxies through **entropic tension**.

### 2.4 Halos as Entropy Regulators
Halos act as stabilizing reservoirs distributing energy flow evenly across cosmic structures.  
They maintain gravitational coherence and regulate entropy progression at galactic scales.

## 3. Mathematical Framework
The Grid evolution follows the thermodynamic field relation:
\[
\nabla \cdot [k(S)\nabla E_f] = \frac{\partial V(E_f, S)}{\partial S}
\]
where \(V(E_f,S)\) describes the potential coupling between energy flow and entropy.  
This replaces Λ as the cause of cosmic acceleration.
